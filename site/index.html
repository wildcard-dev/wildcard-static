The Warning Signs
We See Every Day

These aren't just problems—they're symptoms of deeper dysfunction. We've seen them all, and we know how to fix them.

"We shipped nothing this quarter."

You've got a team. You've got funding. But somehow, nothing is getting delivered. Sprints come and go with nothing to show for them.

"Our investors are asking questions."

You're burning through runway, milestones keep slipping, and the next board meeting is going to be uncomfortable. You need a plan that works.

"Everyone's busy, but nothing moves."

Your team is working hard—or at least looks like it. But the product isn't progressing. There's always a blocker, always a delay.

"We're waiting on Amanda's design."

For three months. Meanwhile, the entire roadmap is stalled. Nobody is making the hard call to break the logjam.

"I don't know why we're stuck."

You've tried everything—new processes, new tools, new people. But you can't pinpoint the actual problem. You need outside eyes.

"This contract might be lost."

You've got a deadline, a deliverable, and no clear path to hit it. The stakes are high, and the team is paralyzed.

We're Your
Coxswain

In rowing, the coxswain is the non-rowing member who steers the boat, calls the strategy, and aligns the crew. They see what others can't—because they're not buried in the work of rowing.

That's what we do for failing product teams.

We're not here to row for you. We're here to steer your boat, align your crew, and get you across the finish line.
